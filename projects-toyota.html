Toyota

Here, I will provide you with an
overview of my consulting project for Toyota.

Summary

Analysis of mobility needs and behavior in nascent urban quarters.

Problem Statement

Toyota wanted to understand how to design mobility concepts for new urban quarters. Specifically, Toyota develops the mobility concept for the new urban quarter "Neulichterfelde" in Berlin and strives to leverage best practices from similar projects. Both the mobility behavior and the mobility needs of current residents of nascent quarters were of high interest for Toyota.

Solution Approach

The following steps were followed to derive holistic recommendations for Toyota:

  • Determination of study design: Quantitative self-administered online survey.
  • Selection of mobility needs and mobility behavior characteristics to be studied.
  • Selection of study sites: Freiham, Munich. Aspern Seestadt, Vienna. HafenCity, Hamburg.
  • Development of questionnaire: 10-minute online questionnair, development included 2 pre-tests with >60 participants.
  • Distribution of the questionnaire: Distributed >3,500 flyers with QR-codes and links to the questionnaire plus outreach via email lists to residents.
  • Data analysis: Analyzed the results of over >350 participants using R, SPSS, and Excel. Including descriptive statistics of usage behavior and cluster analysis of residents based on mobility needs (PCA, EFA, Ward's method, and k-means).
  • Results presentation: After communicating on a bi-weekly basis over half a year with Toyota, I gave a final presentation to Toyota FS's senior management including their CEO.
  • Results write-up: A >50 pages report handed over to Toyota for documentation.

Outcome

The major outcome was an in-depth analysis of the mobility behavior of current residents in nascent urban quarters (mode usage, multi-modal choices, vehicle ownership, perception of different modes, commuting behavior, etc.). Furthermore, I identified five distinct clusters based on the resident's mobility needs. These clusters will be used by Toyota for service development and marketing activities.

Tools Used

R, SPSS, Excel, PowerPoint, Word, Unipark (online survey tool).
